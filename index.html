A Cinematic Cartography

This project maps and analyzes the real locations of the Nouvelle Vague within the urban fabric of Paris, connecting physical space to the scenes that redefined global cinema. Through our interactive platform, users can explore the overlap between real Parisian topography and its transfiguration on the big screen.

The Road as Set

In the late 1950s, young French auteurs broke away from traditional studio systems and artificial lighting. Filmmakers like Truffaut, Godard, and Varda took to the streets of Paris with lightweight cameras, transforming the city from a mere decorative backdrop into an active, rhythm-dictating character.

Exploration Tools

The site's architecture offers multiple levels of interaction with geo-cinematographic data. The analytical catalog connects directly with the interactive map, allowing users to follow curated thematic itineraries or explore the historical evolution of the movement through an integrated chronological timeline.
